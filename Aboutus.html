Places To Visit


In Mathura:


  • Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple: This temple is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Krishna. It's a significant pilgrimage site and a place of immense devotion.

  • Dwarkadhish Temple: Dedicated to Lord Krishna, this temple is an architectural marvel and an important religious site in Mathura.

  • Vishram Ghat: This ghat on the banks of the Yamuna River is said to be the spot where Lord Krishna rested after defeating the demon Kansa. It's a popular place for pilgrims to take a dip in the holy river.

  • Gita Mandir: The temple houses a beautiful marble statue of Lord Krishna delivering the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna, a significant moment in Hindu scripture.

In Vrindavan:


  • Banke Bihari Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna in his child-like form. The deity is known for its unique appearance and is considered very auspicious.

  • Prem Mandir: A relatively new addition to Vrindavan's landscape, this temple complex features intricate carvings and statues depicting the various stages of Lord Krishna's life.

  • ISKCON Temple: The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) has a significant presence in Vrindavan. Their temple is a hub of devotional activities and spiritual teachings.

  • Radha Vallabh Temple: Dedicated to Radha Krishna, this temple is known for its devotional singing (bhajans) and is an important center of the Vaishnav tradition.

  • Seva Kunj and Nidhuban: Believed to be the groves where Lord Krishna performed his pastimes with Radha and his devotees, this serene area is beautifully maintained.

  • Nidhivan: A sacred forest where it is believed that Lord Krishna performs his nightly 'Rasa Lila' dance with Radha and other gopis.

  • Govind Dev Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and is a peaceful place for prayer and meditation.

  • Kesi Ghat: Another important ghat on the Yamuna River, associated with the episode of Lord Krishna's victory over the demon Kesi.
